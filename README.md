A data analytics project that evaluates vendor performance across multiple dimensions — sales contribution, delivery efficiency, and customer ratings. The goal is to help businesses identify reliable suppliers and make data-driven decisions for better supply chain management.

---

## Problem Statement

Companies working with multiple vendors often lack visibility into who is actually performing well. Without structured analysis, poor vendor choices lead to delivery delays and supply chain inefficiencies. This project surfaces actionable insights to improve vendor selection and management.

---

## Dataset

The dataset captures vendor-level transactional and operational data:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Vendor ID | Unique identifier for each vendor |
| Product Category | Type of product supplied |
| Order Quantity | Volume of orders placed |
| Sales Amount | Revenue generated per vendor |
| Delivery Time | Time taken to fulfill orders |
| Vendor Rating | Customer/operational rating score |

---

## Tech Stack

- **SQL** — Data extraction and querying
- **Python** — Data cleaning and preprocessing (`pandas`, `numpy`, `matplotlib`)
- **Power BI** — Interactive dashboard and visualization

---

## Analysis Performed

- Total sales generated by each vendor
- Vendor-to-vendor performance comparison
- Delivery delay detection and trend analysis
- Vendor rating distribution and correlation with sales
- Identification of top-performing vendors

---

## Power BI Dashboard

An interactive dashboard provides a consolidated view of vendor health, including:

- Sales by vendor (bar chart)
- Vendor rating comparison
- Delivery delay analysis over time
- Top vendors by total sales

---

## Key Insights

- A small group of vendors contributes a disproportionately large share of total sales.
- Certain vendors exhibit consistently higher delivery delays, creating risk in the supply chain.
- Vendors with higher ratings show a strong correlation with better sales performance and reliability.
